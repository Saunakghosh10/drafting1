Terms and Conditions Template

Use our terms and conditions agreement to create terms of use for your website or app.

Updated September 26, 2023 | Legally reviewed by Brooke Davis

Terms and conditions (or terms of use) are rules a user must agree to to use your website, mobile app, software, or other services. Creating terms of use is essential for any business to protect itself from users who may try to misuse or abuse your services.

What Are the Terms and Conditions?

Terms and conditions (or terms of use) are written rules and regulations between the user and the company that the user must agree to follow to use the company’s website and services. They keep companies safe by informing users of their policies.

A simple terms and conditions agreement will identify the following essential elements:

  • Company: the name and address of the company providing the services or product
  • Website: the company’s website or application that the user will be accessing
  • Service: what service or product is the company providing
  • Rules: the rules and regulations for using the service or product
  • Acknowledgment: an acceptance and acknowledgment by the user to abide by the rules and regulations when using the service or product

The company can tailor the rules and regulations or “terms of use” according to the service or product it provides and its specific needs.

Privacy Policy vs. Terms and Conditions

A privacy policy is a separate policy from the terms and conditions agreement. It describes how a company collects, uses, maintains, and discloses personal information and data from its customers. Most countries require any website that collects and stores any personal information from its customers to have a privacy policy.

Meanwhile, a terms of use agreement is optional, as it defines individualized rules for website use.

Why Use a Terms and Conditions Agreement

Terms and conditions protect the company and act as an instruction manual for its website. It provides the following benefits:

Benefits of a terms and conditions agreement

  • Ensures users interact with the website as intended
  • Helps you minimize profit losses, warranty issues, and misrepresented products
  • Helps you establish your intellectual property rights
  • Helps you build trust with consumers
  • Helps you prevent disputes and resolve conflicts

Common Uses for a Terms of Use Agreement

Almost every website or application that provides a service or product can implement a terms of use agreement. Here are some examples of websites and apps that use one:

  • E-commerce companies
  • Social media websites or applications
  • Search engines
  • Websites or applications providing a service or product
  • Gaming websites or applications

What to Include in Your Terms and Conditions

Terms and conditions should generally have at least the following:

  • Who is the company providing the service or product
  • What is the service or product the company is providing
  • Where is the company’s website
  • When will the agreement terminate
  • Why might the user not be granted access to the website
  • How does the user agree to accept the terms of service

It may also include the following provisions if they apply to your website or application:

General Policies

Some general policies you can include are the following:

  • No Representations: The company makes no representations about the appropriateness or availability of the website in other countries.
  • Minors: Minors are not permitted to use the website or must first receive permission from a parent.
  • Purchasers and Payment: How purchases, billing, and payments on the website will be made
  • Refund and Return Policy: If and how users can return products or receive a refund.

User Representations

Note the following sections about user representations:

  • Registration: The user promises that all the information provided at registration is accurate and will be updated if necessary.
  • Username: The company can change a username if deemed inappropriate.
  • User Content: The user owns or has licenses to any contributions, and the contributions do not infringe on any intellectual property.
  • Contribution License: The user grants the company a license to use contributions.
  • User Reviews: Provides specific guidelines for user reviews.

Other Media

You may clarify how a user interacts with a website or application on different devices or mediums:

  • Mobile Applications: License and terms specific to the mobile app.
  • Social Media: Policies regarding linking social media accounts.

User Behavior

Some sections that can cover user behavior in a terms and conditions agreement include the following:

  • Submissions: The company can use user-submitted questions, ideas, or information about the website as they like.
  • Prohibited Activities: The user cannot use the website for certain activities.
  • Electronic Contracting: The user agrees to the terms of use with any electronic submissions.
  • Electronic Signatures: Users can provide electronic signatures.

Company Rights

Some company rights to include in your terms and conditions include the following:

  • Intellectual Property: All content on the website and trademarks, service marks, and logos are owned and licensed by the company and provided “as is.”
  • Third-Party Websites: The company is not responsible for any third-party websites that it may link to.
  • Site Management: The company may monitor and remove content from the website.
  • User Data: The company may retain specific personal data provided to the website.

Company Policies

Some company policies to note include the following:

  • Privacy Policy: You can reference and link to any company’s privacy policy.
  • Copyright Infringement: Note how users may inform the company of infringements.
  • Counter Notification: Note how users may inform the company of content that the user believes was wrongly taken down from the website.

Company Safeguards

Some safeguards your company may consider implementing include the following:

  • Disclaimers: Any disclaimers by the company regarding the contents and use of the website.
  • Limitations on Liability: Any limitations on the liabilities of the company.
  • Indemnity: The user will indemnify the company in certain situations, such as lawsuits arising from user contributions or the user’s breach of the terms and conditions.
  • Corrections: The company may make corrections to the website or application as it sees fit.

About the Agreement

Additional information to include in your terms and conditions agreement can consist of the following:

  • Term and Termination: How either party can terminate the agreement.
  • Modifications: The company can make modifications to its terms of service.
  • Disputes: The governing law and how disputes will be handled.
  • Notices: Where to send notices.
  • Contact Information: How to contact the company.

How to Enforce Your Terms of Use

Where you place your terms of use on your website can affect its enforceability. Consider enforcing your terms and conditions with either a browsewrap or clickwrap consent method:

Where to Place Your Terms of Use

In addition to using the browsewrap or clickwrap consent method, you can display your terms of use on various website sections. For example, you may consider putting them on your website:

  • Privacy center
  • Payment page
  • User account creation page
  • Footer
